A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that can be scanned using a smartphone camera to quickly access information. It can store various types of data including URLs, contact information, text, and more.

You can generate unlimited QR codes with your account. There are no restrictions on the number of QR codes you can create.

  • PNG: Best for digital use and web display
  • SVG: Vector format, ideal for scalable graphics and print
  • PDF: Good for documents and professional printing

Error correction allows QR codes to be read even if they're partially damaged or obscured. Higher levels of error correction make the code more reliable but also larger. We recommend High (30%) for codes with logos.

You can edit the name, description, and category of a QR code, but the QR code image itself cannot be modified. To change the content or appearance, you'll need to generate a new QR code.

Simply open your smartphone camera app and point it at the QR code. Most modern smartphones will automatically detect and scan the code, displaying the encoded information or offering to save the contact details.

vCard is a standard format for electronic business cards. When someone scans a vCard QR code, they can instantly save the contact information to their phone's address book.
